    \[\begin{alignat}{2} \A_\tot &= \A_\ind + \A_\ext \qquad && \qquad \E = -\nabla \varphi -\del_t \A \\ \varphi_\tot &= \varphi_\ind + \varphi_\ext && \qquad \B = \nabla \times \A \end{alignat}\]

    \[\begin{alignat}{2} \P &= -\eps_0 \E_\ind \qquad && \qquad \M = \mu_0^{-1} \B_\ind \\ \D &= +\eps_0 \E_\ext && \qquad \H = \mu_0^{-1} \B_\ext \\ \E &= \E_\tot && \qquad \B = \B_\tot \end{alignat}\]

    Fundamental Response Tensor (1)

    Basis of (linear)   Response Theory:

    Induced fields can be regarded as functionals of external ones.

    Q:

    Which functional is the "correct" one?

    \(\E_\ind [\E_\ext, \B_\ext], \quad \vcj_\ind[\E_\ext], \quad \B_\ind[\rho_\ext, \vcj_\ext], \; \ldots \; ?\)

    A:

    Postulate of Functional Approach: \(\;\; j^\mu_\ind = j^\mu_\ind[A^\nu_\ext]\)

    Linear approximation: \(\boxed{ \quad \chimn(x, x') = \dfrac{\delta j^\mu_\ind(x)}{\delta A^\nu_\ext(x')} \quad}\)

    Lorentz 4-vector notation:

    \( \quad A^\mu = \pmat{\varphi / c \\ \A} \,, \quad j^\mu = \pmat{c \rho \\ \vcj} \,, \quad x^\mu = \pmat{ct \\ \vcx} \)

    2nd order tensor   \(\chimn\)   contains complete information on EM response.

    Fundamental Response Tensor (2)

    Continuity equation and gauge invariance imply the constraints:

    \[\del_\mu \chimn(x, x') = \del^{'\nu} \chimn(x, x') = 0\]

    ⇒   at most 9 independent linear response functions!

    \[\begin{align} \quad x^\mu &= \pmat{ct \\ \vcx} \\[1.5ex] \quad \del^\mu &= \pmat{ -\del_t \\ \nabla} \\[1.5ex] \quad k^\mu &= \pmat{ \omega/c \\ \vck} \end{align}\]

    For homogeneous systems:       \(\chi(x, x') = \chi(x - x')\)

    Equivalently in Fourier space:   \(\chi(k, k') = \chi(k) \; \delta(k-k')\)  

    this implies in
    3+1 formalism

    \( \quad \chimn(\vck,\omega) = \begin{pmatrix} -\dfrac{c^2}{\omega^2} \, \vck^\T \, \green{\boldsymbol{\tsr\chi}} \, \vck & \dfrac{c}{\omega} \, \vck^\T \, \green{\boldsymbol{\tsr\chi}} \, \\[1.5ex] -\dfrac{c}{\omega} \, \, \green{\boldsymbol{\tsr\chi}} \, \vck & \, \green{\boldsymbol{\tsr\chi}} \, \end{pmatrix} \quad \) with \(\quad \gbox{\; \tsr\chi = \rlap{\phantom{\Huge\langle_{a_a}}} \dfrac{\delta\vcj_\ind}{\delta\A_\ext} \;} \)

    Remaining components form the 3x3   Current Response Tensor   \(\tsr\chi(\vck,\omega)\).

    Universal Response Relations

    Central Claim:
    Current response tensor \(\tsr\chi\)

    materials properties.

    Kubo-Greenwood Formalism (1)

    Q:

    How to calculate response functions from ab initio?

    A:

    Use Kubo Formula in spectral representation.

    Given a (weak) time-dependent perturbation \(X(t)\) that couples to an operator \(\hat{O}_1\) in the Hamiltonian

    \(\Ham = \Ham_0 + X(t) \h \hat{O}_1 \quad \Rightarrow \i\hbar\frac{\de}{\de t} \ket{\Psi(t)} = \Ham(t) \ket{\Psi(t)}\)

    The expectation value of an observable is given by \(A(t)[X] = \braket{\Psi(t) | \hat{O}_2}{\Psi(t)} \)

    Kubo formula allows to express the retarted response of the observable w.r.t. the perturbation solely in terms of unperturbed quantities

    \[\chi^\tns{R}_\AB(t-t') \eqdef -\frac{\i}{\hbar} \, \Theta(t-t') \, \braket{\Psi_0 | \hll{[} \hat{A}_\tn{I}(t), \hat{B}_\tn{I}(t') \hll{]}}{\Psi_0}\]

    Kubo-Greenwood Formalism (1)

    Q:

    How to calculate response functions from ab initio?

    A:

    Use Kubo Formula in spectral representation.

    Kubo Formula for retarded response functions, observable \(\hat{A}\) and perturbation \(\hat{B}\) …​

    \[\chi^\tns{R}_\AB(t-t') \eqdef -\frac{\i}{\hbar} \, \Theta(t-t') \, \braket{\Psi_0 | \hll{[} \hat{A}_\tn{I}(t), \hat{B}_\tn{I}(t') \hll{]}}{\Psi_0}\]

    …​ with time dependence given by Interaction Picture (\(\Ham_0\) → Hamiltonian w/o perturbation)

    \[\hat{A}_\tn{I}(t) = \e^{\i \Ham_0 t / \hbar} \h \hat{A} \h \e^{-\i \Ham_0 t / \hbar}\]

    For non-interacting systems, wavefunctions can be chosen as Slater determinants

    \( \ket{\Psi^N} = \ket{\tn{SL}(\varphi_1, \ldots, \varphi_N)} \) \( \quad \Rightarrow \quad \Ham {}^N = \sum_N \Ham {}^1 \) \( \quad \Rightarrow \quad \Ham {}^1 \ket{\varphi_i} = \epsilon_i \ket{\varphi_i} \)

    FEG convenient b/c allows to work with single-particle equations.

    Kubo-Greenwood Formalism (2)

    In Fourier space and Thermodynamic Limit, Kubo formula reverts to spectral representation

    \( \chi_\AB^\tns{R}(\omega) = \sum\limits_{i,j=0}^\infty \dfrac{ \Big( f_{\beta,\mu}(\epsilon_i) - f_{\beta,\mu}(\epsilon_j) \Big) \h A_{ij} \h B_{ji} }{\hbar(\omega + \i\eta) - (\epsilon_j - \epsilon_i)} \qquad \)

    where \( \qquad A_{ij} = \braket{\varphi_i | \hat{A}}{\varphi_j} \qquad \) and \( \qquad f_{\beta,\mu}(\epsilon_i) = \dfrac{1}{1+ \e^{\beta(\epsilon_i -\mu)}} \)

    In plane waves basis …​

    \( \quad \varphi_\vck(\vcx) = \braket{\vcx}{\vck} = \dfrac{1}{(2\pi)^{3/2}} \, \e^{\i \vck \cdot \vcx} \quad \)

    …​ and for density response

    \( \quad \begin{align} \hat{A} \mapsto \hat\rho(\vcx) &= (-e) \h \hat\psi {}^\dagger(\vcx) \h \hat\psi(\vcx) \\ \hat{B} \mapsto \hat\rho(\vcx) &= (-e) \h \hat\psi {}^\dagger(\vcx') \h \hat\psi(\vcx') \end{align} \)

    Kubo formula yields                   \( \varepsilon_\vck = \frac{\hbar^2 |\vck|^2}{2m} \,,\quad f_\vck = f(\epsilon_\vck) \)

    \[\chi_\rho(\vcq,\omega) = 2 \h e^2 \idkpi \dfrac{f_\vck - f_{\vck+\vcq}}{\hbar(\omega + \i\eta) + \epsilon_\vck - \epsilon_{\vck+\vcq}}\]

    ⇒   famous Lindhard density response

    Full current operator (1)

    For current response we need \(\vcjh\) instead of \(\hat\rho\) from QFT.

    1. Start with free Hamiltonian

      \( \i\hbar \h\del_t \psi(\vcx,t) = \Ham \h \psi(\vcx,t) \,,\quad \Ham = \dfrac{|\hat\vcp|^2}{2m} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \vcj = \dfrac{(-e)\hbar}{2m\i} \Big(\psi^* (\nabla \psi) - (\nabla \psi)^* \psi \Big) \)

    2. Extend with U(1) gauge transformation  →  minimal-coupling

    \( \left. \begin{align} \del_t &\mapsto \del_t - \frac{\i}{\hbar} \, e \varphi \\ \nabla &\mapsto \nabla + \frac{\i}{\hbar} \, e \A \\ \end{align} \;\; \right\} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \vcj = \underbrace{ \dfrac{(-e)\hbar}{2m\i} \Big(\psi^* (\nabla \psi) - (\nabla \psi)^* \psi \Big) }_{\text{orbital}} + \underbrace{\dfrac{e}{m} \, \rho \A}_{\text{diamagnetic}} \)

    ⇒ Both satisfy the Continuity Equation \(\del_t \rho + \nabla \cdot \vcj = 0\)

    Full Current Operator (2)

    The Pauli Equation is of Schrödinger-type and incorporates spin.

    \( \Ham_\tn{Pauli} = \dfrac{1}{2m} \left( \vc{\sigma} \cdot (\hat{\vcp} + e\A) \right)^2 - e \varphi \,, \quad \qquad \vc{\sigma} = (\sigma_1, \sigma_2, \sigma_3)^\T \quad \; \)


    Leads to a total current operator of the form: \( \quad \vcjh = \vcjh_\tn{dia} + \underbrace{\vcjh_\tn{orb} + \vcjh_\tn{spin}}_{\tn{"paramagnetic"}} \)

    with the additional spinorial contribution

    \( \vcjh_\tn{dia} = \dfrac{e}{m} \, \hat\rho \A \,,\qquad \vcjh_\tn{orb} = \dfrac{(-e)\hbar}{2m\i} \Big(\psih {}^\dagger (\nabla \psih) - (\nabla \psih)^\dagger \psih \Big) \)

    \( \boxed{ \vcjh_\tn{spin} = \dfrac{(-e)\hbar}{2m} \, \nabla \times \left( \sum\limits_{s,s'=\uparrow, \downarrow} \psih{}^\dagger_s \, \vc{\sigma}_{s's} \, \psih_{s'} \right) } \)

    This is the most general yet
    non-relativistic current.

    → can also be derived from
    Dirac equation in non-relativ. limit

    Full Current Response Tensor

    Use Generalized Kubo Formula for the current response tensor

    \[\tsr\chi(\vcx,t; \vcx',t') = \boxed{ \frac{\phantom{i}\!\!e}{m} \, \rho(\vcx) \h \delta^3(\vcx-\vcx') \h \delta(t-t') \h \identity } + \boxed{ \frac{\i}{\hbar} \, \Theta(t-t') \, \Big\langle \hll{[} \vcjh_\tn{p}(\vcx,t), \vcjh_\tn{p}(\vcx', t') \hll{]} \Big\rangle }\]

    \( [\hat{A}, \hat{B}] := \hat{A}\hat{B} - \hat{B}\hat{A} \\ \vcjh_\tn{p} = \vcjh_\dia + \vcjh_\orb \)

    Because of bilinearity, commutator yields three contributions
    \[\chi = \chi^\tn{dia} + {\large\purple{(}} \chi^\tn{orb} + \chi^\tn{spin} + \chi^\tn{cross} {\large\purple{)}}\]
    \[\begin{align} \chi^\cdia &= \;\text{local contribution (left box above)} \\[1ex] % \chi^\corb &\ppt \Big\langle [ \vcjh_\corb, \vcjh_\corb' ]\Big \rangle \\[1ex] % \chi^\cspin &\ppt \Big\langle [ \vcjh_\cspin, \vcjh_\cspin' ] \Big \rangle \\[1ex] % \chi^\ccross &\ppt \Big\langle [ \vcjh_\corb, \vcjh_\cspin' ] + [ \vcjh_\cspin', \vcjh_\corb ] \Big\rangle \end{align}\]

    known:   diamagnetic + orbital
    novel:   spin + cross-correlation

    but:   cross-correlation vanishes for
    non-spin-polarized systems like FEG
